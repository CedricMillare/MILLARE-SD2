Ada Lovelace

Software Engineer

Ada Lovelace was a 19th-century English mathematician now celebrated as the world’s first computer programmer. While working with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine, she authored a set of notes that contained the first complex algorithm intended for a machine. She was uniquely visionary, realizing that computers could do more than just crunch numbers; she predicted they could create music and art if given the right data. Lovelace referred to her style of thinking as "poetical science," blending her father Lord Byron’s creativity with her mother’s mathematical discipline. Despite her massive contributions, her work remained largely obscure until the mid-20th century when it inspired early pioneers of modern computing. Today, she is a global icon for women in STEM, with an annual day and a programming language named in her honor.
